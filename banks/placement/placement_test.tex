\begin{titlebox}
\textbf{How to Use This Placement Test}

This assessment is designed to estimate a student's best starting point among Algebra I, Geometry, Algebra II, Precalculus, and Calculus-readiness coursework. The total score gives a strong first recommendation, but the final decision should also be based on the student's pattern of strengths and weaknesses.

\textbf{Recommended process:}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=0.25em]
\item Check the total score range.
\item Review which section(s) caused the score drop.
\item Consider whether mistakes were conceptual or careless.
\item Use teacher judgment before finalizing placement.
\end{enumerate}
\end{titlebox}

\begin{titlebox}
\textbf{Section-by-Section Meaning}

\textbf{Part I --- Foundations and Algebra Skills (Q1--15)}  
Measures factoring, equations, lines, systems, algebraic simplification, and core symbolic fluency.

\textbf{Part II --- Exponents, Rational Expressions, and Functions (Q16--30)}  
Measures exponent rules, rational expressions, function substitution, inverse-style algebra, and abstract manipulation.

\textbf{Part III --- Graphs, Geometry, and Radical Functions (Q31--45)}  
Measures graph recognition, transformations, radicals, absolute value, logs, and basic geometric reasoning.

\textbf{Part IV --- Functions, Trigonometry, and Precalculus (Q46--60)}  
Measures trigonometric identities, function models, inverse functions, domain analysis, and precalculus readiness.

\textbf{Part V --- Geometry, Solid Geometry, and Advanced Readiness (Q61--70)}  
Measures area, volume, circle geometry, similarity, spatial reasoning, and late-stage readiness.
\end{titlebox}

\begin{titlebox}
\textbf{Detailed Placement Notes}

\textbf{0--18: Algebra I}\\
Student likely needs support with core algebra skills such as solving equations, factoring, linear relationships, and symbolic manipulation. This range usually indicates that moving directly into Geometry or Algebra II would be too fast.

\textbf{19--31: Geometry}\\
Student shows partial algebra readiness but may still have inconsistency in symbolic work. Geometry is often appropriate when basic algebra exists but abstract function work and higher-level manipulation are not yet stable.

\textbf{32--46: Algebra II}\\
Student demonstrates reasonable fluency with foundational algebra and some success with functions, exponents, and rational expressions. However, graph transformations, trigonometry, and higher-level function analysis may still be too weak for Precalculus.

\textbf{47--60: Precalculus}\\
Student is generally strong in algebra and functions, and can handle a meaningful portion of the trigonometry and advanced topics. Precalculus is the most appropriate course when the student is not yet consistently strong enough across all higher-level topics to be considered fully Calculus-ready.

\textbf{61--70: Calculus Readiness}\\
Student demonstrates strong command of algebra, functions, graph analysis, trigonometry, and geometry. This range suggests readiness for a calculus-track course, assuming school grades, work quality, and pacing also support that recommendation.
\end{titlebox}
